08 — What feeds phash / ax_hash, and is the AX tree normalized?

Spec clarification Resolved
Affects: REQ-RECORD-003 (dedupe), REQ-RECORD-004 (store if only one changes); concept §8

What's unclear

Dedupe stores nothing iff phash is within phash_threshold (Hamming distance) of the last frame AND ax_hash is identical. Two things are underspecified:

phash

dHash via imagehash, presumably over the full screenshot. What needs confirmation: bit length, and that phash_threshold (default 4) is Hamming distance on that hash. Also: multi-monitor — hashed per-screen or combined? (See [22].)

ax_hash — the critical one

"AX-tree equality" via an exact hash of the AX JSON. Is the JSON normalized first? Real AX trees carry volatile attributes — focus ring, caret/selection, cursor position, scroll offset, timestamps, window IDs, live clocks. If any of these feed the hash, two visually identical frames will never match and dedupe will never fire.

Why it matters

This is the difference between dedupe working at all and it being effectively dead. It also makes REQ-RECORD-003/004 reproducible: to assert "dedupe fired" deterministically you must know exactly which inputs are hashed.

Options / suspected answer

Suspected Hash a normalized projection of the AX tree: keep stable structure, roles, labels, and geometry buckets; strip volatile fields including focus state, selection/caret, cursor position, scroll offsets, timestamps, and ephemeral IDs. The exact strip list is part of the contract and must be documented — it determines what "screen unchanged" means.

For phash: dHash of the rendered screenshot at a fixed downscale; threshold 4 is Hamming distance. Multi-monitor handling needs a decision (per-screen or combined).

Resolution

Decided

  • phash: 64-bit dHash (imagehash) of the active screen's screenshot at a fixed downscale; phash_threshold (default 4) = Hamming distance. Captures target the active display, so multi-monitor needs no combine rule.
  • ax_hash: hash of a normalized projection of the AX JSON. Keep: structure, roles, labels/titles, and coarse geometry buckets (bounding boxes rounded to a grid). Strip (contractual): focus/focused state, selection & caret/insertion point, cursor position, scroll offsets, live value churn (timestamps/clocks), and ephemeral window/element IDs. This strip-list is the definition of "screen unchanged."

Rationale: the user chose "ignore trivial churn," so dedupe must hash a stable projection or it would never fire. No extra sensitivity knob beyond phash_threshold was requested.
